The Astronauts

NA
Mission Commander
Neil Armstrong
Man who first walked on the Moon’s surface. On July 20, 1969, Armstrong alighted from the Eagle lunar module and landed on the lunar surface, uttering his famous line that went down in history.
"That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."
BA
Lunar Module Pilot
Buzz Aldrin
The second man ever to set foot on the lunar surface, along with Armstrong. Aldrin performed some experiments, collected samples from soil and rocks, and even placed the American flag there.
Described the lunar landscape as "magnificent desolation."
MC
Command Module Pilot
Michael Collins
Flew the Columbia command module around the moon, while his fellow astronauts were on its surface. The so-called "forgotten astronaut" was responsible for keeping their ride back home intact.
Orbited the Moon alone — further from any other human than anyone in history.
Scientific Achievements

What Apollo 11 Discovered

🪨
Lunar Rock Samples
Armstrong and Aldrin collected 47.5 pounds (21.5 kg) of lunar rocks and soil — material that remains scientifically invaluable over 50 years later, revealing the Moon's formation history.
🌑
Lunar Surface Geology
Photographs and direct study confirmed the Moon has no active geology, water, or atmosphere. The surface is covered in fine regolith created by billions of years of meteor impacts.
📡
Seismic Experiments
The crew deployed a passive seismometer that recorded seismic activity on the Moon — "moonquakes" — giving scientists their first real-time data about the lunar interior.
🔬
Solar Wind Composition
A foil sheet deployed on the surface captured solar wind particles for the first time. Analysis revealed the composition of gases the Sun constantly streams into space.
🪞
Laser Ranging Reflector
A retroreflector array placed on the surface still allows scientists to measure the exact distance to the Moon to within millimeters using laser pulses — in use to this day.
🚀
Technology Advances
Apollo 11 accelerated development of integrated circuits, fuel cells, lightweight materials, and advanced computing — technologies that transformed modern life far beyond space exploration.
Legacy

Why Apollo 11 Was Valuable

It has been demonstrated by the Apollo 11 space mission that the impossible can actually happen. Mankind can aspire to set extremely ambitious goals and realize them through the help of science, bravery, and determination.

On July 20, 1969, the Moon landing event was watched by an estimated 650 million people across the globe — about one fifth of the world population at that time. It was not only the United States of America who could take pride in this amazing feat but the whole of humankind. It has demonstrated that with imagination and determination, human experience boundaries can go farther than we ever expected. It gave rise to the epoch of scientific cooperation between countries of the world, inspired millions of scientists and explorers, and significantly modified the views on our existence in the Universe.

Personal Reflection

My Thoughts on Space Exploration

My Thoughts

Space exploration is one of the most significant achievements we as humans are capable of undertaking, in my opinion. With the successful completion of the Apollo 11 mission, humanity demonstrated its capability of achieving greatness whenever we dedicate ourselves to realizing a vision that once seemed impossible. We find ourselves in yet another pivotal moment where we venture out into the cosmos, setting our sights on Mars and the Moon. By engaging in such projects, we not only advance in technology and gain new knowledge about the world around us but also become connected by the spirit of discovery that transcends borders. Exploration is part of our nature as human beings, and I strongly feel that we are compelled to undertake further journeys into the unknown.

— Brent Schiltz · Apollo 11 Project
